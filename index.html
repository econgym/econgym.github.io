Track 01

Household Behavior Simulation

Participants design decision-making strategies for heterogeneous household agents in EconGym. Under a given macroeconomic policy, the goal is to make the population-level behavioral distribution produced by 10,000 agents match real U.S. household survey data (Survey of Consumer Finances) as closely as possible — through dynamic simulation.

Problem Background

Why This Challenge Matters

Accurately simulating the economic behavior of large-scale heterogeneous individuals — consumption, savings, labor supply, and investment — is the foundation of macroeconomic analysis and policy evaluation.

However, current AI methods perform poorly on this task. As shown in the EconGym paper:

  • ✗ Simple Behavior Cloning still exhibits significant distributional bias at scale.
  • ✗ Pure RL and pure LLM approaches can even cause economic collapse in complex environments.

This competition focuses on this foundational open problem, and invites the community to jointly explore better methods for individual behavior modeling.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is EconGym?

EconGym is a standardized simulation environment for economic AI agents, supporting heterogeneous households, firms, and policymakers under realistic macroeconomic dynamics.

Which AI methods are allowed?

All paradigms are welcome — LLM agents, reinforcement learning policies, rule-based / classical economic models, or any hybrid combination.

What data is used as ground truth?

The U.S. Federal Reserve's Survey of Consumer Finances (SCF), which provides detailed distributional information on household balance sheets and behaviors.

How will submissions be evaluated?

By distributional distance metrics between the simulated 10,000-agent population and the SCF benchmark. Exact metrics will be published with the starter kit.

Coming soon. Subscribe via Discord to get notified.
